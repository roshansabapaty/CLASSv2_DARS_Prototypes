# CaseIQ — Pitch Script

**Duration:** 3-4 minutes
**Audience:** Leadership, Engineering Partners, Legal/Compliance stakeholders
**Tone:** Confident, clear, human-centered. Not a feature tour — a vision for how the layers connect.

---

## The Script

Every day, Microsoft receives legal demands from law enforcement agencies around the world — court orders, warrants, subpoenas, emergency requests involving child safety, terrorism, and threats to life. Our response specialists are the people standing between those demands and Microsoft's user data. They validate that requests are legally sufficient. They pull the right data from the right services. They deliver it before deadlines expire.

And right now, they're doing all of that while fighting their own tools.

They embed shorthand codes in case titles because the system can't classify cases. They manually filter through hundreds of rows to find what to work on. Their managers build assignment decks in PowerPoint every Tuesday because there's no system to tell them where the backlog is — until it's already a crisis.

**CaseIQ changes that.**

CaseIQ is not a single feature. It's a layered intelligence system where each layer builds on the one beneath it — and the whole thing gets smarter as it runs.

**The first layer is the Case Health Index — CHI.**

Every case gets a deterministic health score — CHI-1 through CHI-5 — calculated automatically from its metadata: what type of legal demand, how many identifiers, which services are involved, when it's due, whether there's a financial penalty, whether it's a child exploitation case. Same inputs always produce the same score. No more inconsistent prioritization. And CHI is dynamic — a routine case auto-escalates as its deadline approaches. No human has to notice. The system notices.

**The second layer is Case List Health.**

Take any set of cases — your active work, a search result, a saved view, an assignment group — and CaseIQ computes the aggregate health of that set instantly. Before you scan a single row, you see: "Warning — 3 CHI-1 unassigned, 2 past due." It's not a report you run. It's the temperature of whatever you're looking at, recalculated every time you change the filter. This is a concept that doesn't exist in any commercial tool we've evaluated.

**The third layer is LENS Health.**

LENS Health takes that same Case List Health calculation and scopes it per assignment group — with thresholds the manager configures, historical trends, and capacity data. How many qualified RS do we have? How fast are cases coming in? Is the backlog growing or shrinking? And when thresholds breach, the system doesn't just show a red indicator — it changes behavior. Surge protocols activate. Focus profiles override. Low-priority work gets deferred. The system adapts to pressure the way an emergency room adapts to a mass casualty event — because we designed it from those same triage frameworks.

**The fourth layer is Legal Demand Intelligence.**

Agencies submit scanned PDFs of physical court orders. Today, a triage specialist reads every page and re-types every field into a form. CaseIQ extracts entities from those documents — agency names, identifiers, date ranges, deadlines, statutory citations — and presents them with confidence scores. The specialist validates instead of re-typing. And every correction they make is a training signal. Over time, extraction accuracy improves per jurisdiction, per agency, per document type. The system learns how to read the demands that our people process every day.

**The fifth layer is forecasting.**

CaseIQ doesn't just tell you where you are. It projects where you'll be in two weeks, four weeks, eight weeks. If David goes on leave next week, how does that affect US Federal capacity? If India volume spikes — as it does every quarter — do we have enough qualified RS? The system answers those questions before the manager has to ask them, and lets them simulate alternatives before committing.

**And running through all of this — from day one — is a complete audit trail.** Every field change, every stage transition, every assignment, every communication. Chain of custody, legally defensible, compliance-ready.

**Now here's what makes this a roadmap and not a wish list.**

These layers are phased so that each one delivers standalone value. Phase 1 gives response specialists a persona-aware case view — triage specialists see triage cases, fulfillment specialists see their work. That alone is an improvement over today. Phase 2 adds CHI scoring and document extraction — now every case is consistently prioritized and the triage form is half-populated before the specialist touches it. Phase 3 adds the compact list view and search. Phase 4 adds assignment groups and focus profiles. Each phase compounds on the last, but none requires all-or-nothing delivery.

And in parallel — because we have no choice — we're running an eEvidence fast lane. EU Regulation 2023/1543 enforces on August 18th. That's not a soft target. CaseIQ's Phase 1 and 2 infrastructure — the persona system, CHI scoring, SLA engine, ETSI intake parser — becomes the foundation for eEvidence compliance. The same system that serves our response specialists also serves the EU regulatory mandate.

**What we're building is not a replacement for CRM.** It's an operational intelligence layer for lawful access response — one that scores every case, computes the health of any view, monitors system-wide operational health, extracts intelligence from legal documents, forecasts staffing needs, and adapts its behavior when the team is under pressure.

We benchmarked this against Kodex — the industry standard used by Meta, Snap, and Coinbase. CaseIQ matches Kodex on every core capability and exceeds it on eight — including CHI scoring, complexity-adjusted capacity planning, surge protocols, staffing forecasts, and document intelligence.

**Our people deserve a system as serious as the work they do.**

That's CaseIQ.
